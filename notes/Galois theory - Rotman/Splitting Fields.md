**Def:** A _splitting field_ of $f(x) \in F[X]$ is a field extension E/F in which f(x) splits while f(x) does not split in any proper subfield of E.

**Example**: If $\omega \neq 1$ is a cube root of unity. then $x^3 - 1 \in \mathbb{Q}[X]$  splits over $\mathbb{C}$ but its splitting field is $\mathbb{Q}(\omega )$ 

**Theorem:** If F is a field then every polynomial f(x) in $F[X]$ has a splitting field.

**Proof:** refer Rotman
