# Homework-6

1. Let $K$ be the splitting field of a polynomial $g(x) \in k[x]$ and $f(x) \in k[x]$ be irreducible. Suppose $f(x) = p(x) q(x) \cdots$, where $p$ and $q$ are irreducible in $K[x]$. Prove that there exists a $k$-automorphism $\sigma: K \to K$ such that $\sigma(p(x)) = q(x)$.

2. Let $E/k$ be an algebraic extension, $\alpha, \beta \in E$. Prove that there exists $\sigma: E \to E$, a $k$-automorphism, with $\sigma(\alpha)=\beta$ $\iff \operatorname{Irr}(\alpha;k) = \operatorname{Irr}(\beta;k)$.

3. Let $K$ be the splitting field of a polynomial $f(x) \in k[x]$ and let $g(x) \in k[x]$ be irreducible. Assume that $g(x)$ has a root in $K$. Prove that $g(x)$ splits over $K$.

4. Let $m$ be an integer that is not a square and let $\alpha = a + b\sqrt{m}$, $a, b \in \mathbb{Q}$, be a root of a polynomial $f(x) \in \mathbb{Q}[x]$. Show that $a - b\sqrt{m}$ is also a root of $f(x)$.

5. Give an infinite field $K$ of positive characteristic $p$ such that the map $\varphi : K \to K$, $x \mapsto x^p$ is not surjective.
