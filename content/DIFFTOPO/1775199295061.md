# Key Lemma Proof – Reparametrisation

## Diffeomorphism $s: I \to J$

Then $s$ gives a diffeomorphism $I \to J$ with inverse $t(s): J \to I$.

Then the reparametrisation
$$J \xrightarrow{t(\cdot)} I \xrightarrow{\alpha(\cdot)} \mathbb{R}^n$$
gives the same image as that of $\alpha(t)$ and has **unit speed** by the chain rule:

$$\frac{d}{ds}\alpha(t(s)) = t'(s)\,\alpha'(t(s)) = \frac{\alpha'(t(s))}{\|\alpha'(t(s))\|}$$

> [!INFO] Key Lemma (restated)
> $X$ = connected 1-manifold. Let $I, J$ be open connected subsets of $\mathbb{H}^1$ (and hence intervals). Let $f: I \to X$ and $g: J \to X$ be diffeomorphic unit-speed parametrisations to open sets in $X$. Then $f(I) \cap g(J)$ has at most 2 connected components. If it has only one connected component, then $f$ and $g$ extend to a unit-speed parametrisation of $f(I) \cup g(J)$. If there are 2 components then $X \cong S^1$.
