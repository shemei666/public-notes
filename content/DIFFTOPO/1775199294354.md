# Proof of Transversal Homotopy Theorem (Continued)

## Proof

> [!NOTE]- Proof
> Define $F(x, s) := \pi(f(x) + \varepsilon(f(x)) \cdot s)$ for $\pi, \varepsilon$ as in the $\varepsilon$-neighbourhood theorem. Clearly, this is a smooth map.
> 
> Also, $F(x, 0) = \pi(f(x)) = f(x)$ since $\pi|_Y$ is identity. Finally, observe that for any fixed $x \in X$, the map $s \mapsto f(x) + \varepsilon(f(x)) \cdot s$ is an affine linear invertible map and hence a submersion. It follows that $dF$ is onto at each point of $\{x\} \times S$ since $\pi$ is also a submersion. In particular, $F$ and $\partial F$ are submersions. $\blacksquare$

---

## Corollary

> [!TIP] Corollary: Transversal Approximation
> For any smooth map $f: X \to Y$ and for any boundaryless $Z \subseteq Y$, $\exists$ a homotopy $f \sim g$ such that $g, \partial g \pitchfork Z$.
