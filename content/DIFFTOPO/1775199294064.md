# Transcription

> [!TIP] Proposition (Inverse function theorem revisited)
> $X \xrightarrow{f} Y$ smooth, $X, Y$ boundaryless, $Z \subseteq X$ boundaryless and
> $Z \xrightarrow{\sim} f(Z)$ a diffeomorphism. If $df_x : T_x X \to T_{f(x)} Y$ is an isomorphism
> for all $x \in Z$, then $f$ maps an open neighborhood of $Z$ in $X$ diffeomorphically
> to an open nbd of $f(Z)$ in $Y$.

> [!TIP] $\varepsilon$-nbd theorem
> $\exists$ a smooth positive function $\varepsilon : Y \to \mathbb{R}_{>0}$ such that $Y^\varepsilon$
> admits a submersion $Y^\varepsilon \xrightarrow{\pi} Y$ which is identity on $Y$. If $Y$ is compact,
> $\varepsilon$ can be chosen to be constant.

> [!NOTE]- Proof
> We may assume w.l.o.g. that $f$ is local diffeomorphism and onto.
> For each $x \in Z$, $\exists\ U_x \subseteq X$ open and $V_x = f(U_x) \subseteq Y$ open with $U_x \simeq V_x$.
> Then use $X_1 = \cup U_x$, $Y_1 = \cup V_x$.
> Now $f$ is also open and onto.
> Next we may assume w.l.o.g. that $Z = f^{-1}f(Z)$.
> $\blacksquare$
