> [!TIP] Lemma: Submanifold Neighborhood
> $Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$ submanifold. Then any open nbd $U$ in $\mathbb{R}^M$ of $Y$ contains $Y^\varepsilon$ for some smooth positive $\varepsilon : Y \to \mathbb{R}$. If $Y$ is compact, then $\varepsilon$ may be chosen to be constant.

> [!NOTE]- Proof
> For each $p \in Y$, choose an open nbd $V_p \subseteq Y$ (open) and a constant $\varepsilon_p > 0$ such that $U_p := V_p^{\varepsilon_p}$ lies in $U$.
> $\blacksquare$

$\exists$ a smooth positive function $\varepsilon : Y \to \mathbb{R}_{>0}$ such that $Y^\varepsilon$ admits a submersion $Y^\varepsilon \xrightarrow{\pi} Y$ which is identity on $Y$. If $Y$ is compact, $\varepsilon$ can be chosen to be constant.
