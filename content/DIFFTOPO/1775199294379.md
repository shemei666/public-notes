# ε-Neighbourhood: Definition and Proposition

## Setup

Here $Y^\varepsilon$ is defined as:

$$Y^\varepsilon = \left\{ p \in \mathbb{R}^M \;\middle|\; d(p, y) < \varepsilon(y) \text{ for some } y \in Y \right\}$$

i.e., the union of $\varepsilon(y)$-balls around $y$ for $y \in Y$.

(Diagram: a tubular neighbourhood around a curve, and an annulus around a circle — both are examples of $Y^\varepsilon$.)

## Proposition (Assuming ε-nbd Theorem)

> [!TIP] Proposition
> Consider $f: X \to Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^N$ smooth, and $Z \subseteq Y$ a submanifold, $Z, Y$ boundaryless. Then $\exists$ a smooth family $F: X \times S \to Y$ for $S \subseteq \mathbb{R}^N$ unit ball such that $F(x, 0) = f(x)$ and $F, \partial F$ are submersions.
