# Transcription

Clearly $Z \subseteq f^{-1}(f(Z))$. Also $f(Z)$ is a manifold (as $Z \simeq f(Z)$)
and hence $f^{-1}f(Z)$ is also a manifold (as $f$ is a submersion).
Since $Z$ & $f^{-1}f(Z)$ have the same dimension (as $f$ is a local diffeo)
therefore $Z$ is an open subset of $f^{-1}f(Z)$, say $Z = X_2 \cap f^{-1}f(Z)$
for $X_2 \subseteq X$ open. Now replace $X$ by $X_2$, $Y$ by $f(X_2)$.

Next we may assume that $X$ is covered by open sets $U_i$
such that $U_i \xrightarrow{\sim} f(U_i) = V_i$ is a diffeomorphism and $V_i$ are locally finite in $Y$.

First find an open cover $\{U_x\}$ giving diffeomorphisms $U_x \xrightarrow{\sim} f(U_x)$.
By partitions of unity $\exists$ a cover $\{V_i\}$ of $Y$ subordinate to $\{V_x\}$.
Now choose $U_i$ inside $U_x \dots \to \overline{V_x}$
If necessary, replace $X$ by $\cup U_i$.

Now consider $W \subseteq Y$ given by $W = \{ y \in Y \mid f^{-1}(y) \text{ is a singleton} \}$.
Clearly $f(Z) \subseteq W$. It suffices for Proposition to show that $W$ is open in $Y$.
