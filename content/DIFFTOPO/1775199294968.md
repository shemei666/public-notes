# Key Lemma Proof – Structure of Γ

## Graph of $\varphi$ as Line Segments

Since $\varphi' \equiv 1$ or $-1$ on each connected component of $U$, we see that $\Gamma$ consists of straight-line segments (open/closed/semi-open) of slope $\pm 1$.

We think of $I \times J$ as a rectangle whose topological boundary (possibly at $\infty$ too) has four "line segments". The following properties hold:

> [!INFO] Property (i)
> If $(s_0, t_0) \in \Gamma$ and $s_0 \notin \partial I$, $t_0 \notin \partial J$, then $(s_0, t_0) \in \operatorname{Int}(\Gamma)$ as $U, V$ are open and $\varphi$ is a diffeomorphism.
