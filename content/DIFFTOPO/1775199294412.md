# Proof of Proposition (Transversal Family via Translation)

> [!NOTE]- Proof
> Let $S$ = unit ball in $\mathbb{R}^N$. Consider the family:
> $$F(x, s) : X \times S \to \mathbb{R}^N, \qquad (x, s) \mapsto f(x) + s$$
> 
> This is a submersion since $x \times S \xrightarrow{F} \mathbb{R}^N$ itself is a submersion $\forall x \in X$.
> 
> Hence $F$ is a submersion; it is transversal to every submanifold of $\mathbb{R}^N$ and hence to $Z$ too. By the Transversal Family Theorem, $f_s$ is transversal for some $s$.
> 
> But clearly $f$ is homotopic to $f_s$, e.g. via the homotopy $f_{ts}$. $\blacksquare$

## Motivation: ε-Neighbourhood Theorem

When $Y$ is not $\mathbb{R}^N$, we need a way to get back to $Y$ after translating.

> [!TIP] ε-Neighbourhood Theorem
> If $Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$ is boundaryless, then $\exists$ a positive smooth function $\varepsilon: Y \to \mathbb{R}$ and a submersion $\pi: Y^\varepsilon \to Y$ which is identity on $Y$.
