# Key Lemma – Statement and Proof Setup

> [!INFO] Key Lemma (full statement)
> Let $X$ be a connected 1-manifold. Let $I, J$ be open connected subsets of $\mathbb{H}^1$ (and hence intervals). Let $f: I \to X$ and $g: J \to X$ be diffeomorphic unit-speed parametrisations to open sets in $X$. Then $f(I) \cap g(J)$ has **at most 2** connected components. If it has only one connected component, then $f$ and $g$ extend to a unit-speed parametrisation of $f(I) \cup g(J)$. If there are 2 components, then $X \cong S^1$.

## Proof Continuation – Boundary Argument

(ii) Since $\Gamma \subseteq I \times J$ is closed, every component of $\Gamma$ "ends" in one of the 4 topological boundaries of $I \times J$, i.e., the projection of any component $\Gamma'$ of $\Gamma$ to $I$ (or $J$) results in a subinterval containing one of the "ends" of $I$ (or $J$).

> [!NOTE]- Else both projections of $\Gamma'$ are bounded. Replace $I, J$ by closures of these projections $I', J'$. Then $\Gamma' \subseteq I' \times J'$ is compact, hence contains an extremal point $(x_0, y_0)$ in $\mathbb{R}^N$ with $x_0 \notin \partial I$ and $y_0 \notin \partial J$.
