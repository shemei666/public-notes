# Key Lemma – Proof (Beginning)

## Setup

**Proof of Key Lemma.** If $f(I) \cap g(J) = \emptyset$, we are done. So assume the intersection is non-empty.

Since $f(I)$ and $g(J)$ are open in $X$, the map
$$\varphi := g^{-1} \circ f$$
on their intersection is a diffeomorphism from an open subset $U \subseteq I$ to an open subset $V \subseteq J$.

## Unit Speed Condition

Then $|\varphi'(s)| \equiv 1$ by the chain rule:
$$f = g \circ \varphi \implies \|f'(s)\| = \|g'(t)\varphi'(s)\| = |\varphi'(s)| \cdot \|g'(t)\|$$

By continuity, on every connected component of $U$: $\varphi' \equiv 1$ or $\varphi' \equiv -1$.

## The Graph $\Gamma$

Consider the graph $\Gamma$ of $\varphi$ as a subset of $I \times J$. Then $\Gamma$ is a **closed** subset of $I \times J$ because it is the locus where the functions $(s,t) \mapsto f(s)$ and $(s,t) \mapsto g(t)$ agree.
