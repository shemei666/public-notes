# Classification of 1-Manifolds: Key Lemma

## Key Lemma

> [!INFO] Lemma
> Let $f: I \to X$, $g: J \to X$ be unit speed (onto open sets). Then $f(I) \cap g(J)$ has at most 2 components.
> - **One component** $\Rightarrow$ $f(I) \cup g(J)$ can be parametrised by extending $f$ or $g$.
> - **Two components** $\Rightarrow$ $X \cong S^1$.

> [!NOTE]- Proof
> We define the map $I \cup J \xrightarrow{\psi} X$ by
> $$t \mapsto \begin{cases} f(t) & t \in I \\ g(t) & t \in J \end{cases}$$
>
> This is well-defined: on $I \cap J$ we have $f(t) = g(t)$, as the graph is a line of slope 1 through the origin ($\varphi(t) = t$).
>
> It is a smooth local diffeomorphism. Moreover, topologically it identifies the interval $(a, b)$ with $(a', b')$. Thus we get a quotient map, for example by using 
> $$t \mapsto \left(\cos\left(\frac{2\pi t}{b-a}\right), \sin\left(\frac{2\pi t}{b-a}\right)\right).$$
