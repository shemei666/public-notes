$Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$ $k$-manifold, $p \in Y$. $N_p(Y) = (T_p Y)^\perp$ in $\mathbb{R}^M$ normal space.
$$ N(Y) \subseteq Y \times \mathbb{R}^M \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{2M} $$
$$ N(Y) = \{ (p, v) \mid p \in Y, v \in N_p(Y) \} $$
$$ \pi: N(Y) \to Y $$
$$ (p, v) \mapsto p $$
is a surjective smooth map.

$V \subseteq Y$ open $\implies N(V)$ open in $N(Y)$ because $N(V) = N(Y) \cap (V \times \mathbb{R}^M)$ open in $N(Y)$.
To check $N(Y)$ is a manifold, we may shrink $Y$ if necessary.

Now assume $Y = g^{-1}(0)$ for $g: U \to \mathbb{R}^{M-k}$, $U \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$ open, $g = (g_1, \dots, g_{M-k})$ independent.
$p \in Y$, $T_p Y = \ker(dg_p)$
$N_p Y = \text{row space of } dg_p$
(the matrix $dg_p$ has size $(M-k) \times M$)

Now consider the function $Y \times \mathbb{R}^{M-k} \to N(Y)$
$$ (p, u) \mapsto (p, dg_p^\top u) $$
