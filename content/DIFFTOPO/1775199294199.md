# Normal Bundle and ε-Neighbourhood Theorem

## The Normal Bundle of $Y$ in $\mathbb{R}^M$

> [!INFO] Definition: Normal Bundle
> The **normal bundle** of $Y$ in $\mathbb{R}^M$ is:
> $$N(Y) := \left\{ (p, v) \;\middle|\; p \in Y,\; v \in N_p(Y) \right\} \subseteq Y \times \mathbb{R}^M$$

There is a natural smooth surjection $N(Y) \to Y$ via projection, whose fiber over any $p \in Y$ is $N_p(Y)$.

If $V \subseteq Y$ is an open set, then:

$$N(V) = N(Y) \cap V \times \mathbb{R}^M$$

which is open in $Y \times \mathbb{R}^M$. Thus $N(V)$ is an open subset of $N(Y)$.

To check that $N(Y)$ is a manifold, we may shrink $Y$ as per convenience.

---

## ε-Neighbourhood Theorem (Setup)

Assuming $Y = \bar{g}^{-1}(0)$ as before, we'll show that (with $k = \dim(Y)$):

$$Y \times \mathbb{R}^{M-k} \longrightarrow N(Y) \quad \text{is a diffeomorphism}$$

via the map:

$$(p, u) \longmapsto \left(p,\, (dg_p)^{\top} u\right)$$

> [!TIP] ε-Neighbourhood Theorem
> If $Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$ is boundaryless, then $\exists$ a positive smooth function $\varepsilon: Y \to \mathbb{R}$ and a submersion $\pi: Y^\varepsilon \to Y$ which is identity on $Y$. If $Y$ is compact, then $\varepsilon$ can be chosen to be constant.
