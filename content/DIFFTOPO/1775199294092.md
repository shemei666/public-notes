**Claim:** This is a diffeomorphism.

**Pf:** Clearly it is smooth. It is bijective as $\text{rank}(dg_p^\perp) = M - k$. To give the inverse map, fix a point $p \in Y$. For simplicity assume that the top $(M-k) \times (M-k)$ submatrix $A_p$ of $dg_p^\perp$ is invertible. Then the same submatrix $A_y$ is invertible for all $y$ in a nbd of $p$. Now consider the map 
$$ (y, A_y^{-1} T u) \longleftarrow (y, u) $$ 
(projects to the first $M-k$ coords) This is smooth in $y$ and we see that it is an inverse $u \mapsto dg_p^\perp u \mapsto A^{-1} T dg_p^\perp u$. Q.E.D.
(where $T: \mathbb{R}^M \to \mathbb{R}^{M-k}$)

**Theorem (Summarizes)**
For $Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$ a $k$-manifold, its normal bundle $N(Y)$ is an $M$-manifold in $\mathbb{R}^{2M}$ and the canonical map
$$ \pi: N(Y) \to Y $$
$$ (p, v) \mapsto p $$
is a submersion.
(locally use $Y \times \mathbb{R}^{M-k} \simeq N(Y)$)
