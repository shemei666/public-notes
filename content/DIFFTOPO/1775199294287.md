# Normal Space and Normal Bundle

## Normal Space at a Point

Let $Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$ be a submanifold. For any $p \in Y$, we define the **normal space** to $Y$ at $p$ to be:

> [!INFO] Definition: Normal Space
> $$N_p(Y) := \text{orthogonal complement of } T_p Y \text{ in } T_p \mathbb{R}^M = \mathbb{R}^M$$

If $\dim(Y) = k$, then $\dim N_p(Y) = M - k$.

---

## Normal Space via Level Sets

Suppose $Y$ = zeros of $M - k$ independent functions $g_i$ on some open set $U \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$. (This is always true locally), i.e. $Y = \bar{g}^{-1}(0)$ for $g: U \to \mathbb{R}^{M-k}$, $g = (g_1, \ldots, g_{M-k})$.

Then for $p \in Y$:

$$T_p Y = \ker(dg_p)$$

while

$$N_p(Y) = \operatorname{span of the rows of } dg_p$$

That is, $N_p(Y) = \{ v \in \mathbb{R}^M \mid dg_p \cdot v = 0 \;\; \forall\, v \in T_p Y \}$.
