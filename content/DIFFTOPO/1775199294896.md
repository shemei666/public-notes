# Key Lemma: Proof (Continued) — 2-Components Case

## Proof (Continued)

> [!NOTE]- Proof (Continued)
> **(iii) continued** Any horizontal/vertical line in $I \times J$ intersects $\Gamma$ in at most one point since $\varphi$ is bijective.
>
> This implies that each boundary of $I \times J$ meets at most one component of $\Gamma$. If two components $\Gamma_1, \Gamma_2$ "meet" a boundary, then any nearby parallel line must intersect both $\Gamma_1, \Gamma_2$ at least once. Since any component can reach at most 2 boundaries (out of 4), $\Gamma$ has at most 2 components.
>
> As $P \to V$ is a diffeomorphism and $V \to g(V) = f(I) \cap g(J)$ is also a diffeomorphism, the first assertion follows.
