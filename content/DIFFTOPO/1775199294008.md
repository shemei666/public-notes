# Transcription

Clearly $|f^{-1}(y)| \ge 1$ $\forall y \in Y$ as $X \xrightarrow{f} Y$ is assumed onto.
We check that $Y \setminus W$ is closed. Pick a sequence $y_k$ in $Y \setminus W$
with $\lim y_k = y \in Y$. Then $\exists$ a compact neighbourhood $B$ of $y$
such that $B \cap \overline{V_i} \neq \emptyset$ only for finitely many $i$. Hence $f^{-1}B$
is compact. $\big[$ If $V_1, \dots, V_n$ are such that $B \cap \overline{V_i} \neq \emptyset$, then $f^{-1}B \cap U_j = \emptyset$
for $j \neq i$. Hence $f^{-1}B = \bigcup_{i=1}^n \text{inverse image of } B \cap \overline{V_i} \text{ in } \overline{U_i} \ (\text{compact}) \big]$ (can be arranged!)

Now for each $y_k$, by assumption $\exists\ x_k \neq \tilde{x}_k$ in $X$ such
that $f(x_k) = y_k = f(\tilde{x}_k)$. As $f^{-1}B$ is compact, $\{x_k\}$ and $\{\tilde{x}_k\}$
have a limit point say $x, \tilde{x}$ resp. in $f^{-1}B$ and we may assume $\lim x_k = x$, $\lim \tilde{x}_k = \tilde{x}$.
Since $f$ is a local diffeomorphism $x \neq \tilde{x}$ [Else in a nbd $x_k$ & $\tilde{x}_k$ go to the same point]
But $f(x) = y = f(\tilde{x})$. $\therefore y \notin W$.
