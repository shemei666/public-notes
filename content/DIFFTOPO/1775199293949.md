# Transcription

Choose a partition of unity $\{\theta_i\}$ in $Y$ subordinate to $\{V_p\}$.
Thus for each $i$, $\exists\ p_i \in Y$ such that $\text{supp}(\theta_i) \subseteq V_{p_i}$. Now
use $\varepsilon := \sum \varepsilon_{p_i} \theta_i$. This sum being locally finite, makes
sense and hence is a positive smooth function on $Y$. To see that
that this $\varepsilon$ works, observe that for any $y \in Y$, $\varepsilon(y) = \sum \varepsilon_{p_i} \theta_i(y) \le \varepsilon_p$ (finite sum for the maximizer of these)
Thus $x \in Y^\varepsilon \implies d(x, y) \le \varepsilon(y)$ for some $y \in Y$
$\implies x \in V_p^{\varepsilon_p}$ where $\varepsilon_p$ is the max for $y \in V_p$
$\implies x \in U$. Thus $Y^\varepsilon \subseteq U$ QED
