# Classification of 1-Manifolds: Proof Conclusion

## Proof (Continued)

We have the commutative diagram:

$$I \cup J \longrightarrow S^1$$

$$\psi \searrow \quad \downarrow \alpha$$

$$\psi(I \cup J)$$

Here both $S^1$ and $\psi(I \cup J)$ are quotient spaces for the same relation. Hence there is a unique homeomorphism between them making the diagram commute.

Since the other two maps are local diffeomorphisms, so is $\alpha$. Since $\alpha$ is bijective it is a diffeomorphism. Since $S^1$ is compact, $\psi(I \cup J)$ is compact, hence closed in $X$. Since $f$ and $g$ are open maps, $\psi(I \cup J)$ is also open in $X$.

Since $X$ is connected:

$$\psi(I \cup J) = X \cong S^1. \quad \blacksquare$$
