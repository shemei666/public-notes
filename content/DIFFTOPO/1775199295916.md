# Transversality with Boundary

> [!INFO] Theorem
> Let $f: X \to Y$ be smooth, $Z \subseteq Y$ submanifold, with $Z, Y$ boundaryless. If $f \pitchfork Z$ and $\partial f \pitchfork Z$, then $f^{-1}Z$ is a manifold with boundary $f^{-1}Z \cap \partial X$ and codimension is $\operatorname{codim}_Y(Z)$.

**Proof:** We reduce it to the above Proposition.
Observe that these statements can be verified locally on $Y$ and also on $X$.
Therefore, we may assume that $Z$ is given by the vanishing of some $l$ independent functions on $Y$, and that $X$ is an open subset, say $U$ in $\mathbb{H}^k$.

$$U \xrightarrow{f} Y \xrightarrow{h} \mathbb{R}^l$$

with $Z = h^{-1}(\vec{0})$.
Now the condition $f \pitchfork Z$ and $\partial f \pitchfork Z$ boils down to $\vec{0}$ being a regular value of $h \circ f$ and $h \circ \partial f$. By the previous proposition, the result follows. Q.E.D.

# Regular Values and Boundaries

> [!INFO] Proposition
> Let $g: U \to \mathbb{R}^l$ be a smooth map where $U$ is open in $\mathbb{H}^k$. If $a \in \mathbb{R}^l$ is regular for $g$ and also for $\partial g$, then $g^{-1}(a)$ is a submanifold of codimension $l$ (if non-empty) with boundary $g^{-1}(a) \cap \partial U$.
