# Transversal Family Theorem & ε-Neighbourhood Theorem

## Transversal Family Theorem

Let $\partial S = \partial Y = \partial Z = \emptyset$.

Consider $F: X \times S \to Y$ with $F, \partial F \pitchfork Z$. Then:

$$f_s, \partial f_s \pitchfork Z \quad \text{for almost all } s \in S$$

In particular, this holds when $F, \partial F$ are submersions.

---

## Homotopy and Transversality

Let $X \xrightarrow{f} \mathbb{R}^N$ with $\partial Z = \emptyset$, and $f \sim g$, $g, \partial g \pitchfork Z$.

Use $F: X \times S \to \mathbb{R}^N$ defined by $(x, s) \mapsto f(x) + s$, with $f = f_0 \sim f_1$ via $f$.

## ε-Neighbourhood Theorem

$\exists$ a smooth positive function $\varepsilon: Y \to \mathbb{R}_{>0}$ such that $Y^\varepsilon$ admits a submersion $Y^\varepsilon \xrightarrow{\pi} Y$ which is identity on $Y$. If $Y$ is compact, $\varepsilon$ can be chosen to be constant.

---

## ε-Neighbourhood Setup

Let $\varepsilon: Y \to \mathbb{R}_{>0}$ be smooth, positive.

$$Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M, \qquad Y^\varepsilon := \left\{ x \in \mathbb{R}^M \;\middle|\; d(x,y) < \varepsilon(y) \text{ for some } y \in Y \right\}$$

$$Y \subseteq Y^\varepsilon \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$$

---

## Assuming ε-Neighbourhood Theorem

**Given** $f: X \to Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$.

By the theorem, we can find a family $F: X \times S \to Y$ with $F, \partial F$ submersions:

$$(\mathbf{x}, s) \mapsto \pi(f(\mathbf{x}) + \varepsilon(f(\mathbf{x})) \cdot s)$$

for $\pi, \varepsilon$ as above.

> [!TIP] Transversal Homotopy Theorem
> $X \xrightarrow{f} Y \subseteq \mathbb{R}^M$, $f \sim g$, where $g, \partial g \pitchfork Z$.
