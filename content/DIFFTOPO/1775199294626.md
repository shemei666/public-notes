# Lemma on Direct Sum Projections and Transversal Family Theorem

## Lemma

> [!INFO] Lemma
> Given a diagram:
> $$T_1 \oplus T_2 \xrightarrow{\psi} V, \quad \pi_2|_{\ker \psi} \text{ onto} \implies \psi|_{T_1} \text{ onto}$$
> where $\pi_1, \pi_2$ are projections from $T_1, T_2$ and $\pi_2|_{\ker \psi}$ is onto.

> [!NOTE]- Proof of Lemma
> Pick $v \in V$ and $(t_1, t_2) \in \psi^{-1}(v)$. As $\pi_2|_{\ker \psi}$ is onto, $\exists\, (s, t_2) \in \ker(\psi)$.
> 
> $\therefore \psi(t_1 - s, 0) = \psi(t_1, t_2) - \psi(s, t_2) = v.$ $\blacksquare$
> 
> OR: check that both conditions are equivalent to $\ker \psi + T_2 = T_1 + T_2$.

## Transversal Family Theorem

> [!TIP] Transversal Family Theorem
> Suppose $F: X \times S \to Y$ and $\partial F$ are transversal to $Z \subseteq Y$ where $\partial S = \partial Y = \partial Z = \emptyset$. Then for almost all $s \in S$, $f_s$ and $\partial f_s$ are transversal to $Z$.
