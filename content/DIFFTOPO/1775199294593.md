# Theorem: Transversal Family → Transversal Fibers

## Proof of the Transversal Family Theorem

> [!NOTE]- Proof
> Let $W := F^{-1}(Z)$. If $W = \emptyset$ we're done. Else, by transversality of $F, \partial F$, $W$ is a manifold with:
> $$\partial W = W \cap \partial(X \times S) = (\partial F)^{-1} Z.$$
> 
> Now consider the composite $W \subseteq X \times S \xrightarrow{\pi_2} S$. By Sard's theorem, almost all $s \in S$ are regular values for this map. We'll now check that any such $s \in S$ works.
> 
> **Transversality for $f_s$:** We check it pointwise. Pick $(x, s) \in f_s^{-1}(Z) \subseteq W$.
> 
> Let $y = F(x, s) = f_s(x)$.
> 
> $$T_{(x,s)}(X \times S) \cong T_x X \oplus T_s S \to T_y Y \twoheadrightarrow \frac{T_y Y}{T_y Z}$$
> 
> (onto as $F$ is transversal at $(x, s)$).
> 
> Since $s \in S$ is a regular value, $T_{(x,s)} W$ projects onto $T_s S$.
> 
> $$\ker Y = T_{(x,s)}(W)$$
