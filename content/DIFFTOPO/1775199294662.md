# Sard's Theorem Application & Lemma on Onto Maps

## Proof (Continued)

> [!NOTE]- Proof
> Using Sard's theorem, pick $p \in \partial X$ that is regular for $g$. Then $p$ is also regular for $\partial g$ as $\partial g = \text{Identity}$.
> 
> Hence $g^{-1}(p)$ is a manifold with boundary $g^{-1}(p) \cap \partial X$ of codimension $k-1$, i.e., of dimension $1$. It is compact since $g^{-1}(p)$ is closed in $X$.
> 
> But $g^{-1}(p) \cap \partial X$ can only be $\{p\}$ since $\partial g = \text{Identity}$.

## Lemma

> [!INFO] Lemma
> Let $\psi: T_1 \oplus T_2 \to V$ be an onto linear map with kernel $K$. Then the restriction of $\pi_2: T_1 \oplus T_2 \to T_2$ to $K$ is onto $\iff \psi|_{T_1}$ is onto.
