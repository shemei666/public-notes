# Normal Bundle: Definition and Properties

## The Normal Bundle of $Y$ in $\mathbb{R}^M$

> [!INFO] Definition: Normal Bundle
> The **normal bundle** of $Y$ in $\mathbb{R}^M$ is:
> $$N(Y) := \left\{ (p, v) \;\middle|\; p \in Y,\; v \in N_p(Y) \right\} \subseteq Y \times \mathbb{R}^M$$

There is a natural smooth surjection $N(Y) \to Y$ via projection, whose fiber over any $p \in Y$ is $N_p(Y)$.

If $V \subseteq Y$ is an open set, then:

$$N(V) = N(Y) \cap V \times \mathbb{R}^M$$

which is open in $Y \times \mathbb{R}^M$. Thus $N(V)$ is an open subset of $N(Y)$.

To check that $N(Y)$ is a manifold, we may shrink $Y$ as per convenience.
