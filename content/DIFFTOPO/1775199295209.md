# Corollary – Intersection of Submanifolds

> [!INFO] Corollary
> Let $Y$ be boundaryless, $X, Z \subseteq Y$ submanifolds with $Z$ boundaryless. Then $X \cap Z$ is a submanifold (if $\operatorname{codim}_Y = \operatorname{codim}_Y X + \operatorname{codim}_Y Z$) with boundary $\partial X \cap Z$, if:
> - For all $p \in X \cap Z$: $T_p X + T_p Z = T_p Y$, and
> - If $p \in \partial X$, then $T_p \partial X + T_p Z = T_p Y$.
>
> *(Use $i: X \hookrightarrow Y$)*

# Sard's Theorem

> [!INFO] Sard's Theorem
> Let $f: X \to Y$ be smooth with $Y$ boundaryless. Then **almost all** $y \in Y$ are regular for $f$ (for $f|_{\partial X}$) and for $\partial f$.
> 
> *(Outside a measure-0 set.)*

## Proof

Consider the critical points for $f|_{\operatorname{Int}(X)}: \operatorname{Int}(X) \to Y$ and $\partial f: \partial X \to Y$. The union of their images has measure 0 by the usual Sard's theorem. If $d(df)_x$ is onto then so is $df_x$. The complement of this is then regular for $f$ and $\partial f$. $\blacksquare$
