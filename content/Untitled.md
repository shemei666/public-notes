## $\mathbb{C}$ Tutorial, 1 April 2026

1. Show that, $\int_0^{\pi} \frac{d\theta}{a+cos\theta} = \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{a^2 - 1}}$ for $a>1$.
2. Find $\int_0^{\pi} \frac{d\theta}{(a + cos\theta)^2}$, for $a>1$.
**(*Answer is $\int_0^{\pi} \frac{d\theta}{(a + b cos\theta)^2} = \frac{a\pi}{(a^2 - b^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}}$ for $a> b$, $a>1$*)**
