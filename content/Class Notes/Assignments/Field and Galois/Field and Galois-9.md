1. Let $K$ be a finite normal extension of $k$ such that there are no proper subextensions of $K/k$. Prove that $[K:k]$ is a prime. Give a counter-example if $K/k$ is not normal.


2. Let $Q$ be the group of quaternions $\{+1, +i, +j, +k\}$. Prove that $Q$ cannot be the Galois group of a degree $4$ polynomial over some field.

3. Let $K/k$ be finite Galois. Prove that $\exists \alpha \in K$ such that $\{\sigma(\alpha) \mid \sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(K/k)\}$ is a $k$-basis of $K$.


4. Let $k$ be a field, $\mathrm{char}(k) \ne 2$, $K/k$ Galois with $[K:k]=4$. Suppose $\mathrm{Gal}(K/k) \simeq \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$. Prove that $\exists a, b \in k$ such that $K = k(\sqrt{a}, \sqrt{b})$.

5. Let $K = \mathrm{Split}(X^4 - 2; \mathbb{Q})$. Find $\mathrm{Gal}(K/\mathbb{Q})$ and all intermediate subfields.

6. Let $K/k$ be finite normal and $L/k$ algebraic. If either $K/k$ or $L/k$ is separable, show that $[KL : L] = [k : k \cap L]$. Give an example to show this is false without the separability assumption.
