# Homework-8

1. Let $L/k$ be a finite extension of fields, let $N \subseteq \overline{k}$ be its normal closure. Prove that $[N:k] < \infty$.

2. Let $L/k$ be a separable extension of fields, admitting only finitely many $k$-embeddings in an algebraic closure $\overline{k}$ of $k$. Prove/disprove $[L:k] < \infty$.

3. Let $f(x) \in \mathbb{Q}[x]$ be irreducible and $L = \operatorname{Split}(f;\mathbb{Q})$. Prove/disprove: $G = \operatorname{Aut}(L)$ acts transitively on the set of roots of $f(x)$ in $L$.

4. Let $\alpha$ be a root of $x^3 + x^2 - 2x - 1 \in \mathbb{Q}[x]$. Verify $\beta = \alpha^2 - 2$ is also a root. Let $L = \mathbb{Q}(\alpha)$. Determine $\operatorname{Aut}(L)$ and show that $L/\mathbb{Q}$ is normal.

5. Prove/disprove: Let $L/k$ be a field extension, $\alpha \in L$ be transcendental over $k$; $f(x) \in k[x]$ be nonconstant. Then $f(x)$ is transcendental over $k$.
    - If $\beta \in L$ be an element with $f(\beta) = \alpha$, then $\beta$ is transcendental over $k$.

6. Construct an infinite tower (sequence) of algebraically closed fields $K_1 \subsetneq K_2 \subsetneq K_3 \subsetneq \cdots$

7. Consider a field extension $k(\alpha, \beta)/k$. Suppose $\alpha \in k$ is algebraic over $k$ and $\beta$ is transcendental over $k$. Can $k(\alpha, \beta)$ be simple over $k$? Explain.

8. Prove/disprove: for a field $K$, the groups $(K, +)$ and $(K^\times, \cdot)$ can never be isomorphic.

9. $R$ be an integral domain containing a field $k$. With the natural vector space structure, suppose $\dim_k R < \infty$. Prove that $R$ is a field.

10. Let $L/\mathbb{Q}$ be a finite extension. Prove or disprove: the torsion subgroup of $(L^\times, \cdot)$ is finite.

11. Let $K/k$ be a field extension, $[K : k] = n$ and $f(x) \in k[x]$ irreducible, $m = \deg(f)$. Prove that if $m$ and $n$ are coprime, then $f$ cannot have a root in $K$.

12. Let $f(x) = a_n x^n + \cdots + a_1 x + a_0 \in \mathbb{Q}[x]$ be irreducible, $n > 1$, $a_n \neq 0$. Assume all roots of $f$ lie on $S^1$, the unit circle in $\mathbb{C}$. Prove that $a_i = a_{n-i}$ for all $i$.
