## Theorems and Corollaries (Field Extensions, Splitting Fields)

---

### Theorem 4.1
Suppose that $L/K$ is an extension, and that the elements $x_1, \ldots, x_n \in L$ are linearly independent over $K$. Let $a_1, \ldots, a_n \in K$. Then $a_1 x_1 + \cdots + a_n x_n = 0$ implies $a_1 = \cdots = a_n = 0$.

---

### Theorem 4.2
Suppose that $L/K$ is an extension, and that $L$ is algebraic over $K$. If $K \subseteq L$ is simple, i.e., $L = K(\alpha)$ for some $\alpha \in L$, then $K[x]/(f(x)) \cong L$, where $f(x)$ is the minimal polynomial of $\alpha$ over $K$.

---

### Theorem 4.3
Suppose that $L/K$ is an extension, and that $L$ is algebraic over $K$. Then $L$ is a simple extension over $K$ if and only if $[L : K]$ is a prime number.

---

### Theorem 4.4
Suppose that $L/K$ is an extension and that $L$ is algebraic over $K$. Then for every finite intermediate field $M$ such that $K \subseteq M \subseteq L$, we have
$$
[L : K] = [L : M][M : K]
$$

---

### Corollary 4.1
If $L/K$ is an extension and $L = K(\alpha)$ for some $\alpha \in L$, and $f(x)$ is the minimal polynomial of $\alpha$ over $K$, then
$$
[L : K] = \deg(f(x))
$$

---

### Theorem 4.5 (Measurements of Algebraic Extensions)
Suppose that $L/K$ is algebraic and that $L_1, L_2$ are extensions of $K$, with $L_1 \subseteq L_2$, and $L_2$ is a monomorphism with the property that $L_1 \subseteq L_2$ for such an $L_2$ we say that $L_1 \subseteq L_2$.

---

### Theorem 7.1 (Splitting fields)
Suppose that $K$ is a field, that $f(x) \in K[x]$, and that $L/K$ is an extension. We say that $L$ is a splitting field extension for $f(x)$ over $K$ when $f(x)$ splits into linear factors over $L$ and $L$ is generated by the roots of $f(x)$.

---

### Theorem 7.2
Suppose that $K$ is a field and $f(x) \in K[x]$ is a nonconstant polynomial. Then there exists a splitting field extension $L/K$ for $f(x)$, and if $L_1/K$ and $L_2/K$ are splitting field extensions for $f(x)$, then $L_1 \cong L_2$ as extensions of $K$.

---

### Corollary 7.1
If $K$ is a field and $f(x) \in K[x]$ is irreducible, then its splitting field $L$ over $K$ has degree at most $n!$, where $n = \deg f(x)$.

---

### Theorem 7.3 (Extension of Monomorphisms)
Suppose that $K$ is a field, $f(x) \in K[x]$ is a simple extension, and $L/K$ is an extension. Suppose that $h : K \to L$ is a monomorphism. Then there exists an extension of $h$ to a monomorphism $h^* : K(\alpha) \to L$.

---

### Corollary 7.2
Suppose that $K$ is a field, $f(x) \in K[x]$ is irreducible, $L/K$ is a splitting field extension for $f(x)$, and $h: K \to L$ is a homomorphism. Then there exists an extension of $h$ to a homomorphism $h^*: L \to L$.

---

### Theorem 5.1 (Eisenstein's Criterion)
Suppose that $R$ is an integral domain and that $f(x) = a_n x^n + \cdots + a_0 \in R[x]$. Suppose that $p$ is a prime element of $R$, $p \mid a_i$ for $i < n$, $p \nmid a_n$, and $p^2 \nmid a_0$. Then $f(x)$ is irreducible over $R$.

---

### Theorem 5.2 (Gauss's Lemma)
Suppose that $R$ is a unique factorization domain, and that $f(x) \in R[x]$ is irreducible over $R$. Then $f(x)$ is irreducible over the field of fractions of $R$.

---

### Theorem 5.3 (Localization Principle)
Suppose that $a(x) \in R[x]$ is irreducible over $R$ and $a(x)$ is a prime element in $R[x]$. Then $a(x)$ is irreducible in $T[x]$ where $T$ is a local ring and $a$ is not a unit.

## Theorems and Corollaries (Algebraic Closure, Normal Extensions, Separability, Automorphisms, Galois Theory, Finite Fields)

---

### Theorem 8.1
Suppose that $L/K$ is an extension. The following are equivalent:
- (i) $L/K$ is an algebraic closure of $K$.
- (ii) $L/K$ is algebraic, and every irreducible $f \in K[x]$ splits over $L$.
- (iii) $L/K$ is algebraic, and $L$ is algebraically closed.

---

### Theorem 8.2
If $M = K(\alpha)$, where $\alpha$ is a root of $f \in K[x]$, then there exists a one-to-one map $\phi : M \to L$ with $\phi(\alpha) = \beta$ if and only if $f(\beta) = 0$.

---

### Theorem 8.3
Suppose that $K_1/K$ and $K_2/K$ are two algebraic closures of $K$. Then there is an isomorphism $K_1 \cong K_2$ over $K$.

---

### Theorem 9.1
Suppose that $L/K$ is a normal extension and that $M$ is an intermediate field. Then $L/M$ is normal if and only if $L/K$ is normal and $M/K$ is normal.

---

### Theorem 9.2
Suppose that $L/K$ is a normal extension and that $M$ is an intermediate field. Then every $K$-automorphism of $L$ permutes the roots of the minimal polynomial of any $\alpha \in L$ over $K$.

---

### Corollary 9.1
If $L/K$ is a normal extension and $M$ is an intermediate field, then $L/M$ is normal.

---

### Theorem 9.3
Suppose that $L/K$ is simple algebraic extension and $f$ is the minimal polynomial of $\alpha$ over $K$. Then $L/K$ is normal if and only if $f$ splits over $L$.

---

### Theorem 10.1 (Separability)
Suppose that $f(x)$ is a non-zero element of $K[x]$ and that $L/K$ is an algebraic extension. Then $f(x)$ has a repeated root in $L$ if and only if $f(x)$ and $f'(x)$ have a non-trivial common factor in $K[x]$.

---

### Theorem 10.2
Suppose that $f(x)$ is a polynomial in $K[x]$ and that either $\operatorname{char} K = 0$ or $\operatorname{char} K = p > 0$ and $f(x)$ is not divisible by $p$. Then $f(x)$ is separable.

---

### Theorem 11.1 (Fixed fields and Galois groups)
Suppose that $L/K$ is a Galois extension and that $H$ is a subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut}(L/K)$. Then $L^H/K$ is a Galois extension and $\operatorname{Gal}(L/L^H) = H$.

---

### Theorem 11.2 (Galois group of a polynomial)
Suppose that $f(x) \in K[x]$ is separable and that $L/K$ is a splitting field extension for $f(x)$. Then $\operatorname{Gal}(L/K)$ acts as a permutation group on the roots of $f(x)$.

---

### Theorem 11.3 (Natural isomorphisms)
Suppose that $K$ is a field, $L/K$ a Galois extension, and $G = \operatorname{Gal}(L/K)$. For every subgroup $H \leq G$, the fixed field $L^H$ is a Galois extension of $K$ with Galois group $G/H$.

---

### Theorem 12.1 (Finite fields)
Let $K$ be a field of positive characteristic $p$, and let $f(x)$ be a monic polynomial of degree $n$ over $K$. Then the splitting field of $f(x)$ over $K$ is a finite field extension of $K$ of degree dividing $n!$.

---

### Corollary 12.1
If $L/K$ is a finite field extension with splitting field $L$ over a prime subfield, then $L/K$ is a Galois extension.

---
## Theorems and Corollaries (Primitive Elements, Finite Fields, Cubics and Quartics, Roots of Unity)

---

### Theorem 13.1 (Criterion in terms of intermediate fields)
Let $L/K$ be a finite extension. $L/K$ is simple if and only if there are only finitely many intermediate fields.

---

### Theorem 13.2 (The theorem of the primitive element)
Suppose that $L/K$ is a finite extension and $K$ is infinite. Then $L = K(\alpha)$ for some $\alpha \in L$.

---

### Corollary (to Theorem 13.2)
Let $L/K$ be a finite separable extension. $L = K(\alpha)$ for some $\alpha \in L$, and $L$ is a splitting field extension for the minimal polynomial of $\alpha$ over $K$.

---

### Theorem 14.1 (The discriminant)
Suppose that $f$ is a separable irreducible monic cubic polynomial in $K[x]$. Then the discriminant is given by
$$
\Delta(f) = \prod_{i < j} (\alpha_i - \alpha_j)^2
$$
where $\alpha_i$ are the roots of $f$ in a splitting field extension $L/K$.

---

### Theorem 14.2 (Quartic polynomials)
Suppose that $f$ is an irreducible monic quartic polynomial in $K[x]$. Let $L$ be the splitting field extension of $f$ over $K$, and let $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_4$ be the roots of $f$ in $L$. Then:
- (i) If $f$ has a repeated root in $L$, then $f$ is inseparable over $K$.
- (ii) If $f$ is separable and $K$ has a square root of the discriminant, then $f$ splits over $K$.

---

### Theorem 15.1 (Cyclotomic polynomials)
Let $K$ be a field and $n$ a positive integer. The $n$-th cyclotomic polynomial $\Phi_n(x)$ is irreducible over $K$.

---

### Theorem 15.2 (Irreducibility)
Let $f(x)$ be a polynomial in $K[x]$ and $p$ a prime such that $f(x)$ is irreducible over $K$. The degree of $\Phi_n(x)$ is equal to $\varphi(n)$, where $\varphi$ is Euler's totient function.

---

### Theorem 15.3 (The Galois group of a cyclotomic polynomial)
Let $\zeta$ be a primitive $n$-th root of unity over $K$, where $n > 2$. The minimal polynomial of $\zeta$ over $K$ is $\Phi_n(x)$, and the Galois group $\operatorname{Gal}(K(\zeta)/K)$ is isomorphic to $(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times$.

---

### Corollary (to Theorem 15.3)
If $p$ is a prime, then $x^p - 1$ splits over $K$, and $\operatorname{Gal}(K(\zeta)/K)$ is cyclic.
