**Def:** A topological space is a n-manifold iff it is separable metrizable space where every point has a neighborhood homeomorphic to an open ball in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$. A 2-manifold is also called a surface.


## The Classification of 1-manifolds

**Theorem:** Suppose M is a compact, connected 1-manifold. Then M is triangulable. i.e. M is homeomorphic to a subset C of $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ consisting of a finite collection of straight line segments where any two segments of C are either disjoint or meet at an endpoint of each.
